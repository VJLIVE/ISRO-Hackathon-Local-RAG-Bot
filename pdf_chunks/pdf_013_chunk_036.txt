Introduction Land surface albedo, defined as the fraction of incident solar radiation (0.3–4.0 um) reflected by land surfaces (Dickinson, 1995; Liang, 2004) is one of the most important parameters in general circulation models, hydrology models, numerical weather models, and surface radiation- budget studies. Land surface albedo (LSA) is a key geophysical parameter controlling the energy budget in land–atmosphere interactions (Dickinson, 1983). LSA varies spatially and evolves seasonally based on solar illumination conditions, rainfall, soil moisture, vegetation growth, snow accumulation/melting and anthropogenic activities.
